We are Lee Street

David Blakely - Guitarist

D.B. (also known as "Pennsylvania Bentley") is our resident blues guitar man. Splitting his childhood between the forests of PA and Trenton, Nj, he learned to blend the rural with the urban and the classic with the modern. A chronic runaway child, his first run-in with the law was on a short-lived trip to Chicago at age 14, being caught after walking over 30 miles. When he was 19-years old, he set out to hop his first train, riding back and forth across the country as far south as Louisiana, and as far north as Michigan (involving at least 6 or 7 less-than-friendly police officers and a sheriff or two). Now 24, he's running away one more time, and spreading his guitar playing all over the world.

DB’s favorite guitarist is Pete Townshend, his favorite album is "Comfort Eagle," and his favorite book is George Orwell’s 1984.

"Surly" Kohl Alexander - Saxophonist

Kohl was born at the confluence of the Mississippi and Missouri Rivers; a haven of blues, jazz, and other eclectic music. It is no surprise that he became musician. He started playing piano, then guitar but, once he got his hands on a saxophone, it was love. With great sax players from St. Louis like Oliver Sain and David Sanborn, it is really no surprise. Kohl grew up listening to the likes of John Coltrane, Buddy Leach, and Grover Washington Sr. He received accolades for musicianship in jazz bands and eventually branched out into playing the blues. Vocally, Kohl learned by singing some of his favorite blues and rock artists such as Howlin' Wolf, Jethro Tull, and Stevie Ray Vaughn.

Kohl's favorite sax player by far is John Coltraine, and his favorite album is "The Complete Columbia Studio Records of John Coltrane and Miles Davis", and his favorite book is a series called "The Jerusalem Quartet" by Edward Whittemore.

"Righteous" Ari Rose - Drummer

Raised on the sun-drenched shores of San Diego, CA, Ari Rose's love for the foreign and unknown has always been boundless. Over the years he's tried out piano and guitar, but the wild energy and pulsing logic of the drums was what really captured his musical passion. He began playing drums and percussion for the award-winning San Diego School of Creative and Performing Arts, and before long began playing rock and roll in a garage band. After leaving music for a number of years, he started drumming for Kohl and D.B. in Tucson, and before long he'd fallen in with their rough-and-tumble blues lifestyle. He had always been such a sweet kid. What the hell happened?

Ari's favorite drummers are Dave Grohl and Keith Moon, his favorite album is Neutral Milk Hotel's "In the Aeroplane Over The Sea," and his favorite book is Neal Stephenson's Cryptonomicon.
