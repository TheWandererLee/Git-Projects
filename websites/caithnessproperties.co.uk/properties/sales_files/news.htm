13/07/2011
MARKET COMMENTARY JULY 2011 At the beginning of this year I gave details of our residential property sales for 2010 which amounted to 50 million pounds, being 193 sales where contracts had been exchanged between January and December with an average selling price of £245,500 per property. As we move into the second half of 2011 conducting the same exercise for the first six months of this year, we have sold i.e, exchanged contracts, on approximately 15 million pounds worth of sales representing 68 properties with an average selling price of approximately £220,000. 2011 has therefore shown a very slow start, partly attributable to the poor weather at the beginning of the year but more to do with the general economic climate and the uncertainty that has prevailed. The first half of 2010 by comparison was far more active with a much higher volume of sales being agreed but the market slowed down in the second half. This year I believe the opposite may apply i.e, poor first half and stronger second half. This seems to be much the case as the last couple of months has seen a considerable improvement to the extent that our pipeline of sales at the 30th June, i.e, the halfway point, showed a further 56 properties under offer but not exchanged with a total sales value of 16 million pounds and an average sale price per property of £287,000. It seems to me that the upturn occurred following the Royal Wedding on the 29th April and a general feeling of well being has emanated from this grand occasion. I am now reasonably hopeful that we will achieve last years volume of sales overall and possibly exceed the 50 million pound total. As estate agents the encouraging factor is that we now have available a much higher number of instructions than at the same time last year giving prospective purchasers a much wider choice in a varied price range at the present time. Obviously a key factor if vendors wish to sell is the pricing of the property correctly in the first place as in general terms prices are flat and not moving either upwards or downwards. Time will tell what happens following the holiday period that we are now entering. Mike Young FRICS

As part of its committment to continually raise the standards its members work to, RICS has launched a monitoring scheme aimed at members working in the valuation sector. The intention of the scheme, known as the Valuer Registration Scheme (VRS), is to ensure that the highest qualified professionals carry out valuations and to maintain an adequate level of public protection by instilling confidence in those who request the services of an RICS valuer. The scheme includes a register of valuers working in RICS regulated firms, such as Michael Graham Young, and other organisations and a system of regulatory monitoring which will ensure compliance with RICS standards.

25/01/2011
As we enter the New Year 2011 I always look back at the results for the previous year to see how we as a business have faired over the previous twelve months in order to take a view going forward as to what we can expect in the current year. Obviously the residential market in Cardiff as with the rest of the UK has been challenging over recent years and has been difficult since September 2007, when the Northern Rock situation arose, followed by the recession. Since that time there has been a fall in prices, although I am of the opinion that in general prices remained fairly static last year. Overall there were not many transactions in 2010 compared with the "heady days prior to 2007". This firm sold, i.e. exchanged contracts, on just under £50 million pounds worth of residential property, namely flats, bungalows and houses between January and December. This represented 199 sales between our three offices covering the whole of Cardiff, giving an average selling price of £245,463. The prices ranged from £86,000 for a retirement apartment in Radyr to £850,000 for a new build five bedroomed family house in Cyncoed and represented sales of four properties being sold a week throughout the year. This factor in itself would dispel any theory that the residential property market was extremely quiet during the last year. It is my opinion that the key factor when selling property is realistic pricing in the first place. It is common knowledge that estate agents tend to over value to gain instructions, as without instructions they have nothing to sell. However, when house prices are static or may be falling, then this works to the disadvantage of the vendor, who in many cases would have achieved a higher price and an earlier sale if they had been more realistic at the outset. The old adage certainly applies that "the best price is usually achieved on a sale of a property in the first few weeks that is placed on the market, and not after the property has been exposed to the market for a year or more. This would appear to certainly apply to properties on the market for in excess of £1,000,000, a figure which appears to be commonly asked in Cardiff but rarely achieved.  Michael G Young FRICS
